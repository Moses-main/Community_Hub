## Executive Summary

This document outlines a comprehensive Go-To-Market (GTM) strategy for CHub - a Church Community Relationship Management platform designed specifically for churches in Lagos, Nigeria. The strategy focuses on sustainable market penetration through relationship-based selling, targeted marketing, and building a network effect among church communities.

With over 20 million people in Lagos and a vibrant church culture, there is significant opportunity for a church management solution that addresses local pain points. This GTM plan provides actionable steps to acquire churches ranging from medium-sized congregations (300 members) to mega churches (5,000+ members).

## Target Market Analysis

### Understanding the Lagos Church Landscape

Lagos is home to some of the largest churches in Africa. Understanding this landscape helps you prioritize your efforts.

### Tiered Target Strategy

#### Tier 1: Mega Churches (5,000+ Members)

**Characteristics:**
- Multiple campuses across Lagos
- Significant budget for technology
- Already aware of digital needs
- Decision-making involves IT teams and leadership
- May already have some form of church management

**Why Target Them:**
- Higher budget, willing to invest in solutions
- Brand association (if they use your product, it signals quality)
- Can become reference customers
- Volume of members creates significant value

**Approach:**
- Executive-level presentations
- Custom demonstrations
- Integration with existing systems
- Competitive pricing for volume

**Example Churches in Lagos:**
- The Light House Church
- Living Faith Church (Winners' Chapel)
- Faith Tabernacle
- Redeemed Christian Church of God (RCCG) zones

---

#### Tier 2: Large Churches (1,000-5,000 Members)

**Characteristics:**
- Growing pains are very real
- Using manual systems or basic Excel
- Looking for solutions to organize operations
- Decision maker is often the pastor directly
- Budget exists but must be justified

**Why Target Them:**
- Highest need - they feel the pain most
- More responsive than mega churches
- Can move faster in decision-making
- Can become advocates in their networks

**Approach:**
- Focus on specific pain points
- Show time/cost savings
- Offer trial periods
- Demonstrate ease of use

---

#### Tier 3: Medium Churches (300-1,000 Members)

**Characteristics:**
- Close-knit community
- Pastor knows most members by name
- Limited budget but eager to grow
- Looking for affordable solutions
- May be on WhatsApp groups together

**Why Target Them:**
- Most numerous - largest market segment
- Quick decision-making
- Word-of-mouth potential in pastor networks
- Lower price point = lower risk for them

**Approach:**
- Emphasize affordability
- Show simplicity and ease of adoption
- Leverage pastor networks for referrals
- Offer grouped pricing for denominational deals

---

## Sales Strategy

### The "Help First, Sell Second" Methodology

Church leaders are skeptical of salespeople. The best approach is to provide value before asking for anything.

### Sales Funnel Overview

```

```

---

### Stage 1: Awareness & Interest Building

**Objective:** Get church leaders to know you and trust you

**Tactics:**
- Provide free content (blog posts, social media tips)
- Engage in conversations on WhatsApp and social media
- Comment on posts by church leaders
- Share relevant industry statistics

**Key Message:**
> "I'm working with churches to help them connect better with their members. Happy to share some insights if helpful."

---

### Stage 2: Free Consultation

**Objective:** Understand their challenges without selling

**Format:** 30-minute discovery call or in-person meeting

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "How do you currently manage attendance on Sundays?"
2. "How do new visitors become registered members?"
3. "What's your process for following up members who stop attending?"
4. "How do members give - is there online giving?"
5. "How do you communicate with your members?"
6. "What's the biggest operational challenge you face?"

**After Discovery:**
- Summarize the gaps you've identified
- Ask: "Would it be helpful if I showed you how CHub addresses this?"
- Only proceed to demo if they express interest

---

### Stage 3: Personalized Demo

**Objective:** Show how CHub solves their specific problems

**Demo Approach - Don't Show Everything:**

| Lead With | Avoid Showing |
|----------|---------------|
| Their pain point #1 | Every single feature |
| What they'll save time on | Complex admin panels |
| Easy onboarding | Technical specifications |
| Testimonial from similar church | Full pricing upfront |

**Demo Structure (15-20 minutes):**

1. **Opening (2 min):** Acknowledge their specific challenges
2. **Solution Demo (10 min):** Show relevant features only
3. **Proof Points (3 min):** Share one relevant case study
4. **Next Steps (2 min):** Offer free trial, answer questions

---

### Stage 4: Free Trial Offer

**Objective:** Remove friction to get them started

**Trial Terms:**
- 30 days free
- Full access to all features
- Dedicated onboarding support via WhatsApp
- No credit card required
- Cancel anytime

**During Trial:**
- Day 1: Send welcome message + quick setup guide
- Day 3: Check in - any questions?
- Day 7: Offer to do a quick training call
- Day 14: Follow up on progress
- Day 25: Discuss conversion - any concerns?

## Handling Objections

### Common Objections and Responses

---

### Objection 1: "We use paper/Excel and it works fine"

**Response:**
> "That's completely understandable. Many churches start with paper and Excel - I respect that.
> 
> The question isn't really about what works today, but what will help you grow tomorrow.
> 
> Let me ask you this: How long does it take to find a specific member's contact information? With CHub, it's instant.
> 
> Would it be helpful to show you a quick comparison? It takes only 5 minutes and you might find some gaps worth addressing."

---

### Objection 2: "We don't have the budget"

**Response:**
> "I understand budget is a consideration. Let me share what I tell other churches: the cost of NOT having a system is often higher than the cost of having one.
> 
> How much time does your team spend on [specific task they mentioned]? If it's 5 hours a week, that's 20 hours a month - time that could go to ministry.
> 
> We also offer a free 30-day trial so you can see the value first before any commitment. What if we started there?"

---

### Objection 3: "Our members aren't tech-savvy"

**Response:**
> "That's a common concern, and I appreciate you thinking of your members.
> 
> The good news is CHub works on any phone - even basic phones. Members don't need a smartphone or app. They just click a link and they're in.
> 
> For the few members who struggle, we provide simple guides. And honestly, most church members in Lagos are already using WhatsApp - they'll adapt quickly.
> 
> Would you like me to show you how simple it is for members to use?"

---

### Objection 4: "We already have a system in place"

**Response:**
> "That's great - it shows you're thinking strategically about church management.
> 
> I'm curious: What's working well with your current system? What would you improve if you could?
> 
> [Listen to their response]
> 
> Would it be helpful to do a quick comparison? I'm not here to badmouth competitors - I just want you to have the best tool for your church. Sometimes a second option helps you negotiate better terms with your current provider."

---

### Objection 5: "I need to pray about it / discuss with my team"

**Response:**
> "Absolutely - this is an important decision and prayer is the right approach.
> 
> Can I send you some materials to share with your team? That way, when you discuss it, everyone has the same information.
> 
> Also, when would be a good time to follow up? I'd love to answer any questions your team has."

---

### Objection 6: "Can you just give it to us for free?"

**Response:**
> "I love your church's heart for serving! Here's what I can offer:
> 
> - We can do a free 30-day trial so you can see the full value
> - If you're a brand new church plant (under 1 year), we have a special church plant package
> - If you can provide a great case study and testimonial, we can discuss special arrangements
> 
> The goal is for this to be sustainable for both of us. What if we started with the free trial and went from there?"
